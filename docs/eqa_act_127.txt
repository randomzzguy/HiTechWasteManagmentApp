ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY ACT 1974 (ACT 127)
Malaysia - Key Provisions for Waste Management

PART I - PRELIMINARY
Section 2 - Interpretation
"Scheduled waste" means any waste prescribed under the Environmental Quality (Scheduled Wastes) Regulations 2005.
"Licensee" means a person who holds a licence issued under this Act.
"Premises" includes any land, building, or structure.

PART IV - PROHIBITION AND CONTROL OF POLLUTION
Section 22 - Prohibition on pollution of inland waters
No person shall, unless licensed, emit, discharge or deposit any environmentally hazardous substances, pollutants or wastes into any inland waters.

Section 23 - Prohibition on pollution of Malaysian waters
No person shall, unless licensed, emit, discharge or deposit any environmentally hazardous substances into Malaysian waters.

Section 24 - Prohibition on pollution of the atmosphere
No person shall, unless licensed, emit, discharge or deposit any environmentally hazardous substances into the atmosphere.

Section 25 - Prohibition on pollution of the soil
No person shall, unless licensed, emit, discharge or deposit any environmentally hazardous substances into or onto any soil.

PART V - SCHEDULED WASTES
Section 34B - Scheduled wastes
(1) No person shall, unless licensed, treat, store, transport or dispose of any scheduled waste.
(2) Any person who contravenes subsection (1) commits an offence and shall, on conviction, be liable to a fine not exceeding five hundred thousand ringgit or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding five years or to both.

SCHEDULED WASTES REGULATIONS 2005 - KEY PROVISIONS:

Regulation 7 - Storage of scheduled wastes
(1) A waste generator shall store scheduled wastes in a storage area that:
(a) is clearly labelled with the scheduled waste code and hazard symbols
(b) has adequate containment to prevent spillage
(c) is secured against unauthorized access
(d) has adequate ventilation

Regulation 8 - 90-Day Storage Rule
(1) A waste generator shall not store scheduled wastes for more than 180 days from the date of generation.
(2) For certain categories (SW 305, SW 410, SW 420), the storage limit is 90 days.
(3) The Director General may grant an extension upon written application.

Regulation 9 - Consignment Note Requirements
(1) No person shall transport scheduled wastes without a consignment note.
(2) The consignment note shall be in the form prescribed in the Third Schedule.
(3) The consignment note shall be submitted to the Director General within 30 days.
