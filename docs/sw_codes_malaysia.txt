MALAYSIA SCHEDULED WASTE CODES - FIRST SCHEDULE
Environmental Quality (Scheduled Wastes) Regulations 2005
Under the Environmental Quality Act 1974 (Act 127)

PART I - SCHEDULED WASTES

SW 102 - Waste that contains cyanide
Description: Waste containing cyanide compounds including plating bath waste, stripping solutions
Physical state: Liquid/Solid
Storage limit: 90 days from generation date
Packaging: Sealed drums, clearly labelled

SW 104 - Waste that contains mercury or mercury compounds
Description: Mercury-contaminated equipment, fluorescent lamps, thermometers, batteries
Physical state: Solid/Liquid
Storage limit: 90 days
Packaging: Double-sealed containers

SW 204 - Waste mineral oil/water mixtures or emulsions
Description: Cutting fluids, rolling emulsions, hydraulic fluid emulsions
Physical state: Liquid
Storage limit: 90 days
Packaging: 200L drums or IBC containers

SW 305 - Used lubricating oil
Description: Used engine oil, hydraulic oil, gear oil, transmission fluid
Physical state: Liquid
Storage limit: 90 days from collection date
Packaging: 200L steel drums, IBC containers
Disposal: Licensed scheduled waste contractor, Kualiti Alam or approved facility
Note: Must not be mixed with other waste streams

SW 306 - Waste fuel (petrol, diesel, fuel oil, spent fuel)
Description: Off-specification fuel, contaminated diesel, spent fuel oil
Physical state: Liquid
Storage limit: 90 days
Packaging: Sealed fuel-grade containers

SW 322 - Waste that contains polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs)
Description: PCB-containing transformers, capacitors, hydraulic fluids
Physical state: Solid/Liquid
Storage limit: 90 days
Special handling: Requires DOE notification before disposal

SW 401 - Clinical waste
Description: Sharps, pathological waste, contaminated dressings, pharmaceutical waste
Physical state: Solid
Storage limit: 90 days (refrigerated storage recommended)
Packaging: Yellow clinical waste bags, sharps containers
Disposal: Incineration at licensed clinical waste facility

SW 408 - Asbestos waste
Description: Asbestos-containing materials, insulation, ceiling tiles
Physical state: Solid
Storage limit: 90 days
Packaging: Double-sealed heavy-duty bags, clearly labelled ASBESTOS

SW 409 - Used tyres
Description: End-of-life tyres from vehicles, machinery
Physical state: Solid
Storage limit: 90 days
Note: Maximum 500 tyres per storage area

SW 410 - Batteries
Description: Lead-acid batteries, lithium batteries, NiCd batteries, alkaline batteries
Physical state: Solid
Storage limit: 90 days
Packaging: Original packaging or sealed containers, terminals protected
Note: Lead-acid batteries must be stored upright, no stacking

SW 420 - Electronic waste (e-waste)
Description: Computers, phones, circuit boards, CRT monitors, printers
Physical state: Solid
Storage limit: 90 days
Packaging: Sealed containers, anti-static bags for circuit boards

SW 422 - Waste photographic chemicals
Description: Developer, fixer, bleach-fix solutions, stop bath
Physical state: Liquid
Storage limit: 90 days
Packaging: Original containers or chemical-resistant drums

SW 440 - Pharmaceutical waste
Description: Expired medicines, cytotoxic drugs, vaccines, controlled substances
Physical state: Solid/Liquid
Storage limit: 90 days
Special handling: Cytotoxic waste requires special handling procedures

SW 501 - Leachate from scheduled waste disposal facility
Description: Landfill leachate, contaminated groundwater
Physical state: Liquid
Storage limit: 90 days

SW 503 - Contaminated soil
Description: Hydrocarbon-contaminated soil from spills, petroleum-contaminated earth
Physical state: Solid
Storage limit: 90 days
Note: Requires characterisation before disposal

COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS:
1. All scheduled waste must be stored in designated storage areas
2. Storage area must be labelled with SW code and hazard symbols
3. 90-day storage limit strictly enforced under EQA Act 127
4. Consignment note (e-SWIS) required for all scheduled waste movements
5. Only licensed contractors may transport scheduled waste
6. Cenviro Sdn Bhd is the primary licensed processor in Malaysia
7. Records must be kept for minimum 3 years
8. DOE inspection may occur at any time

PENALTIES:
- Exceeding 90-day storage limit: Fine up to RM500,000 or imprisonment up to 5 years
- Improper disposal: Fine up to RM500,000 or imprisonment up to 5 years
- Failure to maintain records: Fine up to RM100,000
